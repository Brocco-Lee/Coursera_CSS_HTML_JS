Notes and thoughts on PHYSIOLOGY OF STRENGTH AND HYPERTROPHY

Key Points on Chapter: What is strength?

  • When training in order to improve strength and planning training routines, it is helpful to understand, how the body will respond to stress.
  • Overall, strength is predicated on a simple equation:
    Strength = Neural adaptations * Muscle cross sectional area
  • There are four factors, that determine the force output of a muscle
    1. cross-sectional area of the muscle
    2. angle of attack on the joint
    3. individual limb length
    4. neural factors
  • we cannot influence angle of muscle insertions or limb length, hence not mentioned in the equation
  • quicker results can be yielded, when focusing on neural factors and muscle mass gains
  • Neural adaptions are an important driver of strength development
  • Hypertrophy (bigger muscles) can be described as the overlap between developing neural adaptions and muscle cross-sectional area
  • The larger the muscle size, the stronger it will be.
